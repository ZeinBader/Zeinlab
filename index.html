Deciphering RNA Modifications in Plants

Integrating epitranscriptomic maps with functional genomics to uncover regulatory mechanisms under stress and development.

Explore Our Work

About Us

We are a multidisciplinary team at the forefront of plant epitranscriptomics. Our mission is to map and functionally characterize RNA modifications—such as m6A, pseudouridine, and 5mC—across model and crop species, leveraging cutting-edge sequencing and computational pipelines.

Research Projects

Epitranscriptome Mapping

Genome-wide profiling of m6A and pseudouridine in rice and Arabidopsis under abiotic stress.

Functional Validation

CRISPR-based editing of writer/eraser enzymes to dissect roles in development and stress responses.

Computational Pipelines

Integrated workflows for direct RNA sequencing analysis and epitranscriptomic data visualization.

Translational Applications

Engineering stress-resilient crops by targeting epitranscriptomic regulators.
